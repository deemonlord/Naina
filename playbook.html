📋 EYLF Learning Outcomes

1

🌟 Identity

Children have a strong sense of identity. They feel safe, secure, and supported while exploring who they are.

Mirror PlayRole PlayEmotion CardsSelf Portraits
2

🤝 Community

Children are connected with and contribute to their world. They explore relationships and feel a sense of belonging.

Washing SocksGroup MusicStory SharingGardening
3

💪 Wellbeing

Children have a strong sense of wellbeing. They take increasing responsibility for their own health and physical wellbeing.

Ball PlayObstacle CourseDanceMud Kitchen
4

🧠 Learning

Children are confident and involved learners. They develop dispositions for learning such as curiosity, cooperation and creativity.

Shape PuzzlesColour SortingWater PlayPlaydough
5

💬 Communication

Children are effective communicators. They interact verbally and non-verbally with others for a range of purposes.

Story TimeFinger PaintingPuppetsSinging

The EYLF (Early Years Learning Framework) guides early childhood educators in Australia.
All activities in this playbook support one or more of these 5 learning outcomes.

📊 Developmental Milestones (23–30 Months)

🖐
Fine Motor

Hand Skills

  • Turns pages one at a time
  • Builds tower of 6–7 blocks
  • Scribbles in circles and lines
  • Turns door handles and lids
  • Strings 4 large beads
  • Snips paper with scissors (with help)
🏃
Gross Motor

Body Movement

  • Runs with more control
  • Kicks a stationary ball
  • Jumps with both feet
  • Walks up/down stairs with support
  • Climbs on and off furniture
  • Stands briefly on one foot
💬
Language

Communication

  • Uses 50+ words
  • Combines 2–3 words in phrases
  • Asks "what" and "where" questions
  • Follows 2-step instructions
  • Names familiar objects and people
  • Strangers understand ~50% of speech
🧠
Cognitive

Thinking

  • Matches shapes and colours
  • Pretend play becoming complex
  • Sorts objects into categories
  • Understanding cause and effect
  • Counts 2–3 objects
  • Completes 3–5 piece puzzles
❤️
Social-Emotional

Feelings

  • Shows empathy toward others
  • Parallel play evolving to cooperative
  • Expresses a wide range of emotions
  • Strong sense of ownership ("mine!")
  • Seeks adult reassurance
  • Beginning to take turns
🌊
Sensory

Processing

  • Explores textures more confidently
  • Tolerates messy play longer
  • Responds to rhythm and music
  • Identifies basic tastes and smells
  • Enjoys water and sand exploration
  • Developing proprioceptive awareness
